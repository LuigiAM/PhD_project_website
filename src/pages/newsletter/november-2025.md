As we head into the final stretch of 2025, MEMoPAD is transitioning from design to deployment. November feels like the shift from "what could this be done?" to "let's test if it works."

** üé¶ Seeing MEMoPAD in a Movie (Sort Of) **
What does wearable emotion monitoring actually look like in daily life? I created an animated story to show how MEMoPAD could help, following Alex from anxiety overwhelm to early pattern detection to peer support. It's the closest I can get to showing MEMoPAD's vision in two minutes.
Watch it at https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=9uJHqtzO6cw and don't hesitate to share your feedback!

** üìÑ Position Paper Now Available **
Our co-design position paper is now published: "Co-Designing MEMoPAD: Multimodal Emotion Monitoring in Clinical Pathways for Anxiety Disorders."
This covers the first three stages of our co-design journey and was presented at the 'Co-Designing Human-Centred AI Technologies for Health and Wellbeing' workshop at HCI 2025.
Thanks to my co-authors Carmel McGrath, Lucy Condon, Miles Thompson, Paul Matthews, Michael Loizou, and David Western; as well as to the workshop organizers for creating space for this work. Read the paper here: https://drive.google.com/file/d/1zStNmaGHpnxizDS_yjEucyk30WLvm1WV/view

** üéì For International Students in Innovation **
Potentially good news for your future in the UK. From 25 November 2025, UK international students can switch to the Innovator Founder visa from inside the UK, provided they have completed their course or 24 months of a PhD. Key requirements include securing an endorsement from an approved body for an innovative, viable, and scalable business, meeting B2 English language and maintenance fund requirements, and demonstrating growth potential. More info: https://www.gov.uk/innovator-founder-visa/switch-to-this-visa
